♟ Chess

How to Play

Controls

  • Move: click one of your pieces (its legal moves appear as dots), then click a highlighted square.
  • A ring around a square means you can capture there.
  • Promotion (house rule): a pawn reaching the far rank may only become a piece type that was captured from you — you reclaim your own lost material. If several are eligible you'll be asked which one; if you haven't lost an eligible piece yet, it becomes a Queen.
  • Use New Game to restart and Undo Move to take back your last move.

The Pieces

♙ Pawn — forward 1 (or 2 on its first move); captures diagonally.

♘ Knight — L-shape; jumps over pieces.

♗ Bishop — any distance diagonally.

♖ Rook — any distance straight.

♕ Queen — straight or diagonal, any distance.

♔ King — one square any direction.

Goal & Special Rules

  • Checkmate the enemy king (attacked with no escape) to win.
  • Check: if your king is attacked you must get out of it.
  • Castling: move your king two squares toward a rook to tuck it away safely (both must be unmoved, the path clear, and the king not moving through check).
  • En passant: a special pawn capture right after an enemy pawn jumps two squares past yours.
  • Stalemate (no legal move but not in check) is a draw.

After the Game

When the game ends, click Review Game to step through every move. The engine grades each one — from Best to Blunder — shows the move it would have preferred, and rates each side's overall accuracy.
